<p><p>Shape-memory polymers are versatile materials that can be triggered to return to their original shape by various stimuli. Temperature-induced shape-memory polymers change shape in response to changes in temperature, while light-induced shape-memory polymers change shape when exposed to specific wavelengths of light. Electricity-induced shape-memory polymers change shape when an electric current is applied. Other types of shape-memory polymers include pH-responsive polymers, which change shape in response to changes in pH, and magnetic shape-memory polymers, which change shape in response to changes in magnetic fields.</p></p>
