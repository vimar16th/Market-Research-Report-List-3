<p><p>Temporary knee spacers are used in knee surgeries to restore joint geometry and maintain range of motion. There are two main types of temporary knee spacers: cement and metal. Cement knee spacers are made from bone cement and are commonly used in cases with significant bone loss. Metal knee spacers, on the other hand, are made of alloys like titanium or cobalt-chromium and are used in cases where bone preservation is crucial. Both types help to maintain joint stability and prevent further damage before a permanent implant is inserted.</p></p>
