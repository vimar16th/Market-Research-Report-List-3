<p><p>Aircraft ice protection systems are crucial for preventing ice formation on the aircraft's surfaces during flight. The types of ice protection systems commonly used in the market include pneumatic de-icing boots, electro-thermal systems, electro-mechanical systems, TKS ice protection, bleed air systems, and passive systems. Pneumatic de-icing boots inflate and deflate to break ice off surfaces, while electro-thermal systems use electric heating elements. Electro-mechanical systems use mechanical devices to remove ice, TKS systems dispense a glycol-based fluid, bleed air systems use hot air to prevent ice buildup, and passive systems rely on the aerodynamic design of the aircraft to reduce ice formation.</p></p>
