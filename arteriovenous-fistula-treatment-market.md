<p><p>Arteriovenous fistula treatment market comprises various types, including dural arteriovenous fistulas, peripheral arteriovenous fistulas, and pial or cerebral arteriovenous fistulas. Dural arteriovenous fistulas occur in the lining of the brain and cause abnormal connections between arteries and veins. Peripheral arteriovenous fistulas occur outside the brain and spinal cord, involving connections between arteries and veins in the limbs. Pial or cerebral arteriovenous fistulas occur within the brain, where abnormal connections develop between arteries and veins. These different types of arteriovenous fistulas require tailored treatment options according to their specific locations and characteristics.</p></p>

Transcatheter embolization involves the insertion of embolic material to block the abnormal connection between arteries and veins. Ultrasound-guided compression uses pressure to close the AVF non-surgically. Surgery is a more invasive option where a surgeon repairs or removes the abnormal connection.
