<p><p>Slime control agents are essential in preventing the growth of unwanted slime in various industrial processes. Chlorine dioxide, fuzzicide, sodium chlorophenate, phenates, and mercurial compounds are different types of slime control agents available in the market. Chlorine dioxide is effective against biofilm formation, while fuzzicide works specifically on fungal slime. Sodium chlorophenate and phenates are commonly used for their broad-spectrum antimicrobial properties. Mercurial compounds are potent slime control agents but are less commonly used due to their toxic nature. Each type of slime control agent has its unique benefits and applications in different industries.</p></p>
