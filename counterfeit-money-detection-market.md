<p><p>Counterfeit money detection market consists of various types of technologies used to identify counterfeit banknotes. Ultraviolet (UV) detection involves the use of UV light to detect specific fluorescent elements in genuine notes. Infrared (IR) detects the presence of specific infrared-sensitive inks. Magnetic detection relies on the presence of magnetic elements in genuine notes. Watermark detection involves examining the unique watermark designs embedded in genuine banknotes. Microprint detection analyzes the tiny printed characters on genuine notes. Other technologies may include hologram detection and color-shifting ink detection, among others. These methods help businesses and individuals protect themselves from counterfeit currency.</p></p>
