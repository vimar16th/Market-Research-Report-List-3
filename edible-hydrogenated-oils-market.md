<p><p>Edible hydrogenated oils are commonly used in food processing and cooking. Partially hydrogenated oils are liquid oils that have been processed to become more solid at room temperature. They are often used in the production of margarine and shortening. Fully hydrogenated oils, on the other hand, are completely solid at room temperature and are used in products like baked goods and snack foods. Both types of hydrogenated oils are popular for their longer shelf life and ability to withstand high temperatures during cooking.</p></p>
