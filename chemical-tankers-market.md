<p><p>Chemical tankers are categorized into three main types based on their size and operation area. Inland chemical tankers typically have a capacity ranging from 1,000 to 4,999 deadweight tons (DWT) and operate on rivers and canals. Coastal chemical tankers fall in the range of 5,000 to 9,999 DWT and mainly operate in coastal waters. Deep-sea chemical tankers, with a capacity of 10,000 to 50,000 DWT, are designed for long-distance voyages across oceans and seas. Each type serves a specific purpose in the transportation of chemicals.</p></p>
