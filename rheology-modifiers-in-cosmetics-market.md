<p><p>Rheology modifiers are substances used in cosmetics to modify the flow and texture of products. There are two main types of rheology modifiers based on their solubility: aqueous and non-aqueous. Aqueous rheology modifiers are used in water-based products like lotions and creams to enhance their spreading and stability. Non-aqueous rheology modifiers are used in oil-based products like foundations and lipsticks to control their thickness and consistency. Both types play a crucial role in achieving the desired texture and performance of cosmetic products.</p></p>
